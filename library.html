Champion Enablement Email

Drafts an email for your champion to send to their boss, summarizing the value and ROI of Bob, making it easy for them to sell internally.

Act as a strategic business advisor. I am the internal champion for the Bob HRIS platform at my company. My boss, the [Boss's Title, e.g., CFO], is very busy and focused on ROI.

Draft a concise email for me to send to my boss that:
1.  Briefly introduces Bob in the context of our key business initiative: [e.g., "improving employee retention"].
2.  Summarizes the top 2-3 value propositions we discussed that directly impact our goals.
3.  Includes the key quantitative outcomes we projected, such as [e.g., "a 15% reduction in time-to-hire" or "an estimated $50k annual savings by automating manual HR tasks"].
4.  Proposes a clear next step, which is a brief 15-minute meeting to present the full business case for Bob.

Make the email easy to read, persuasive, and focused on business outcomes, not just features.

Company & Persona Research Brief

Generates a concise pre-call brief on a target company and key stakeholder.

Act as a market research analyst for Bob, a leading HRIS platform. I am preparing for a discovery call.

Generate a one-page brief based on the following information:
- Company Website: [e.g., www.examplecorp.com]
- Target Persona: [e.g., "VP of People"]

The brief should contain four sections:
1.  **Company Overview:** What do they do? What is their industry?
2.  **Recent News & Initiatives:** Any recent funding, product launches, or hiring trends related to HR and people operations?
3.  **Likely Persona Pains:** Based on their role and industry, what are 3-5 challenges this person likely faces that Bob can address (e.g., inefficient onboarding, lack of HR data, poor employee engagement)?
4.  **Strategic Questions:** Suggest 3 open-ended questions I can ask to uncover these pains.

Tailored Demo Script Creator

Takes an uploaded discovery call transcript and generates a structured, value-oriented demo script for Bob.

You are a senior Sales Engineer for the Bob HRIS platform. Based on the provided discovery call transcript file, create a tailored demo flow that tells a compelling story.

The output should be a markdown list. For each step in the demo, include:
-   **The "Why":** State the prospect's pain point you are about to address (e.g., "You mentioned your team struggles with manual performance reviews...").
-   **The "What":** Describe the specific feature in Bob you will show (e.g., "...so let's look at our automated performance review cycle builder.").
-   **The "So What":** Explain the business value for the prospect (e.g., "This will save your HR team dozens of hours per quarter and provide managers with consistent data.").

Cross-Deal Trend Analysis

Analyzes multiple call transcripts to identify strategic trends, common objections, and feature gaps across a specific segment.

Act as a strategic sales analyst. I have uploaded 5 call transcripts from recent deals in the [e.g., manufacturing] sector.

Analyze all transcripts and provide a summary of strategic insights in a markdown table with two columns: "Finding" and "Recommendation".

Your findings should include:
- The top 3 most common objections raised.
- The top 2 most requested features that Bob does not currently have.
- A recurring theme or pain point mentioned in at least 3 of the 5 calls.

For each finding, provide a brief, actionable recommendation.

Integration Value Proposition

Explains the business value of a technical integration to a non-technical audience, focusing on outcomes over mechanics.

I need to explain the value of our integration between Bob and [Partner Name, e.g., Greenhouse].

Draft a short paragraph for a non-technical HR leader. The explanation must:
1. Avoid technical jargon like "API" or "endpoint".
2. Focus on the business outcome (e.g., saving time, reducing errors, improving candidate experience).
3. Use a simple analogy to make the concept clear.

Customer Story Extractor

Analyzes a case study or call transcript to pull out a compelling, concise customer story that can be used in future demos.

Analyze the attached customer case study or call transcript. My goal is to extract a concise and powerful customer story that I can use as an anecdote in future demos.

The output should be a short paragraph that follows the "Challenge, Solution, Result" framework:
1.  **Challenge:** What was the customer's primary pain point before Bob?
2.  **Solution:** What key part of the Bob platform did they use to solve it?
3.  **Result:** What was the measurable or qualitative outcome they achieved?
